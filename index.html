Who We Are

A School Rooted in
Peace & Purpose

Asomdwee Ntonton Som-School is built on the spiritual foundation of Asomdwee Ntonton Som — the Religion of Peace, Praise and Togetherness — a Ghanaian indigenous faith rooted in the worship of the Most High God, RABBI. Our school carries forward the philosophy of its name: weaving unity, discipline and spiritual consciousness into every dimension of learning.

Located in Apam, Central Region, we are committed to providing a holistic, high-quality education that honours both our cultural roots and the aspirations of a modern world. Every child who walks through our doors is seen, valued and empowered.

Our Spiritual Foundation

Ɔkronkronyi Nyame Somafo Yawoh

Founder of Asomdwee Ntonton Som · Spiritual Leader

Asomdwee Ntonton Som-School draws its identity and founding spirit from the life and teachings of Ɔkronkronyi Nyame Somafo Yawoh — founder and spiritual leader of Asomdwee Ntonton Som, widely described as the Third Religion of the Most High God, RABBI. Born on February 28, 1952 in Ekumfi Dunkwa in Ghana's Central Region, his journey into ministry was, as his followers insist, divinely orchestrated.

Described as a messenger, counsellor and reformer, his teachings centre on spiritual awakening, moral renewal and the pursuit of divine peace. He is credited with constructing the Third Temple of God in Ekumfi Otuam and has been received by heads of state including former President John Agyekum Kufuor, in recognition of his contributions to faith and national life in Ghana.

The school carries forward his central conviction: that knowledge, rooted in faith and character, is the most powerful gift a society can offer its children. The name Asomdwee Ntonton — "Threads of Peace" — reflects our philosophy of weaving unity, discipline and spiritual consciousness into every dimension of education.
