Who We Are

A School Rooted in
Peace & Purpose

Asomdwee Ntonton Som School is built on the spiritual foundation of Asomdwee Ntonton Som — the Religion of Peace, Praise and Togetherness — a Ghanaian indigenous faith rooted in the worship of the Most High God, RABBI. Our school carries forward the philosophy of its name: weaving unity, discipline and spiritual consciousness into every dimension of learning.

Located in Apam, Central Region, we are committed to providing a holistic, high-quality education that honours both our cultural roots and the aspirations of a modern world. Every child who walks through our doors is seen, valued and empowered.

Our Story

Rooted in Purpose,
Growing with Vision

In 2023, something remarkable took root in Apam. Asomdwee Ntonton Som School opened its doors — not simply as another school, but as a declaration: that every child in this community deserves an education that sees them wholly, honours who they are and prepares them courageously for the world ahead.

The school was conceived and founded in the spirit and image of Asomdwee Ntonton Som — the Religion of Peaceful Exaltations. From its very first day, the school has been shaped by the faith's core conviction: that peace, praise and togetherness are not just virtues to preach, but principles to live and to teach. This is a school where the spiritual and the academic walk hand in hand.

While firmly grounded in the structure and standards of the Ghana Education Service, Asomdwee Ntonton Som School weaves a thread of the religion's teachings into the fabric of daily learning. Children here do not only gain knowledge — they gain wisdom. They are not only taught to read the world; they are taught to understand it with grace, integrity and a sense of divine purpose.

Since 2023, the growth has been both quiet and extraordinary. What began as a bold community vision has blossomed into something genuinely promising — a school that families believe in, children thrive in and teachers pour their hearts into. The journey has only just begun, and already, its promise shines clearly in the lives of every child who has come through.

Our Spiritual Foundation

Ɔkronkronyi Nyame Somafo Yawoh

Founder of Asomdwee Ntonton Som · Spiritual Leader

Asomdwee Ntonton Som School draws its identity and founding spirit from the life and extraordinary works of Ɔkronkronyi Nyame Somafo Yawoh — founder and spiritual leader of Asomdwee Ntonton Som, widely described as the Third Religion of the Most High God, RABBI. Born on February 28, 1952 in Ekumfi Dunkwa in Ghana's Central Region, his calling into ministry was, as his followers insist, not of his own choosing — it was divinely ordained.

What makes his story truly remarkable goes beyond preaching and teaching. Ɔkronkronyi Nyame Somafo Yawoh is widely known among his followers for healing the sick — not through any form of medicine or conventional treatment, but through divine power alone. People from all walks of life, carrying ailments that the world had given up on, have testified to receiving healing through his intercession. To those who have witnessed it, this is not legend — it is lived experience.

He is also the man behind the construction of the Third Temple of God, built in Ekumfi Otuam in Ghana's Central Region — a sacred site that believers from across the country and beyond make pilgrimage to. According to his teachings, Ghana holds a divine place at the centre of the world's spiritual geography, and the Third Temple stands as a fulfilment of ancient prophecy. Ɔkronkronyi Nyame Somafo Yawoh has been received by heads of state, including former President John Agyekum Kufuor, in recognition of his remarkable contributions to Ghanaian faith, culture and national life.

The school carries forward his deepest conviction: that true knowledge, when rooted in faith and shaped by good character, is the most powerful and lasting gift a society can offer its children. The name Asomdwee Ntonton — "Peaceful Exaltations" — reflects our commitment to nurturing young people who live in peace, reach upward in praise and go forward with purpose.

The Common Core Programme (CCP)

Developed by the National Council for Curriculum and Assessment (NaCCA) under the Ministry of Education, the CCP is Ghana's modern, standards-based curriculum for KG through JHS. It focuses on building character, nurturing values and equipping learners with 21st-century skills — going beyond memorisation to foster creativity, critical thinking and real-world problem-solving. Asomdwee Ntonton Som School is fully aligned with this curriculum at all levels.

01

Pre-School

Kindergarten 1 & 2 · Ages 4–6 · 2 Years

The Pre-school years are where everything begins. At Asomdwee Ntonton Som School, we treat these two years — Kindergarten 1 and Kindergarten 2 — with the reverence they deserve, because we understand that what a child experiences in their earliest school years shapes how they feel about learning for the rest of their life.

Our Pre-school follows the GES/NaCCA standards-based curriculum for Kindergarten, which is structured around six core developmental areas. Through play, song, storytelling, movement and guided discovery, our young learners are gently introduced to language, numbers and the world around them — all while developing the social and emotional foundations they will carry into every classroom that follows.

The classrooms are warm and welcoming, our teachers are patient and encouraging, and every child is met exactly where they are. We believe that the best thing a school can do for a four-year-old is to make them love coming to school — and at Asomdwee Ntonton Som School, that is exactly what we do.

02

Primary School

Basic 1 – Basic 6 · Ages 6–12 · 6 Years

Primary School is where curiosity takes flight. Across six years — from Basic 1 through to Basic 6 — our pupils move from learning to read to reading to learn; from counting on their fingers to solving problems with confidence. It is a journey of remarkable transformation, and we have the privilege of witnessing it every single day.

Our Primary curriculum is built on the NaCCA Standards-Based Curriculum introduced in 2019, which divides the six years into Lower Primary (Basic 1–3) and Upper Primary (Basic 4–6). The emphasis throughout is on enquiry, creativity and making meaning — not rote repetition. Children are encouraged to ask questions, think critically and connect what they learn in the classroom to the world outside it.

At Asomdwee Ntonton Som School, Primary education is also where the values of the school are most deeply felt. The gentle integration of the teachings of Asomdwee Ntonton Som — peace, purpose and togetherness — runs quietly through the fabric of daily school life, shaping young minds and young hearts in equal measure.

03

Junior High School

JHS 1 – JHS 3 (Basic 7 – Basic 9) · Ages 12–15 · 3 Years

Junior High School is where students begin to discover who they are becoming. Across three years — JHS 1, JHS 2 and JHS 3 — our students grow rapidly in academic depth, personal maturity and a clearer sense of their own gifts and ambitions. It is our responsibility to match that growth with teaching that challenges, inspires and prepares.

Our JHS programme runs on the Common Core Programme (CCP) Curriculum — Ghana's most recent and comprehensive curriculum reform, implemented progressively from the 2021/2022 academic year. The CCP is designed specifically for Basic 7 to Basic 9, with a focus on building character alongside academic knowledge. It equips learners with 21st-century competencies: critical thinking, communication, creativity, collaboration and digital literacy. There are no optional subjects — every learner studies the full range of core areas.

JHS 3 culminates in the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE), which opens the door to Senior High School. At Asomdwee Ntonton Som School, we prepare our JHS students thoroughly — academically, spiritually and emotionally — so that they leave us ready not just for the exam, but for the life that comes after it.
